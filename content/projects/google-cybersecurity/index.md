*Below: the blank controls & compliance checklist template (top) and my completed assessment for Botium Toys (bottom).*

![Controls and compliance checklist — blank template and completed Botium Toys audit](/uploads/cyber-security-audit.jpg)

This portfolio project placed me in the role of a security analyst at Botium Toys, a fictional small business with a growing online presence. The scenario required reviewing the company's scope of assets, existing security goals, and a risk assessment report that flagged an overall risk score of 8 out of 10, with potential fines for non-compliance with U.S. and international regulations.

**What I did:**
- Reviewed the full risk assessment against NIST CSF guidelines
- Evaluated whether each security control was in place or absent (yes/no per control)
- Assessed compliance with PCI DSS, GDPR, and SOC standards
- Documented findings in a controls and compliance checklist
- Identified critical gaps including lack of encryption for customer credit card data, no intrusion detection system, and inadequate physical access controls
- Completed a self-assessment evaluating the quality of my analysis

**Skills this reflects:**
The activity directly demonstrates the ability to apply the NIST Cybersecurity Framework, understand the CIA triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability), and map organizational risk posture against regulatory compliance requirements. It also reflects familiarity with the CISSP 8 security domains and the kind of structured audit thinking needed for roles in security analysis, GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance), and IT audit support.

### Portfolio Activity: Use the NIST Cybersecurity Framework to Respond to a Security Incident

*Grade: 100% | Submitted: April 20, 2026*

*Below: the blank NIST CSF incident report template (top) and my completed analysis of the ICMP flood DoS attack (bottom).*

![NIST CSF incident report — blank template and completed ICMP flood DoS analysis](/uploads/cyber-nist-incident.jpg)

This capstone activity placed me as a cybersecurity analyst at a multimedia company that had just experienced a Denial of Service (DoS) attack via an ICMP flood through an unconfigured firewall. The attack disabled internal network services for two hours. My task was to create a professional incident report structured around the five NIST CSF core functions.

**What I did:**
- Identified the attack vector: a malicious actor exploited an unconfigured firewall, sending a flood of ICMP packets that overwhelmed the network
- Documented the Protect phase response: new firewall rate-limiting rules, IDS/IPS deployment, and network service segmentation
- Described the Detect actions: source IP verification, abnormal traffic monitoring
- Outlined Respond and Recover procedures: blocking offending traffic, restoring critical services, and communicating with stakeholders
- Produced a structured incident report suitable for a security operations portfolio

**Skills this reflects:**
Applying the NIST CSF to a live-scenario incident demonstrates real readiness for SOC analyst and security engineer roles. It shows an ability to reason across the full incident lifecycle from threat identification through recovery, and to communicate findings in the structured format that enterprise security teams actually use.
